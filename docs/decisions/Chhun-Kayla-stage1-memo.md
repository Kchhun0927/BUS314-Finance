## Executive Summary (150 words max)

This report outlines a financial health assessment of Apple Inc., a global technology company known for products like the iPhone, Mac, and digital services. The goal is to evaluate the company’s recent financial performance using accounting ratio analysis.

Ratio analysis is important because it turns raw financial data into clear insights about how well a company is performing. It helps identify strengths, risks, and trends that are not obvious from financial statements alone. This analysis will focus on six key areas: performance, profitability, efficiency, leverage, liquidity, and Du Pont analysis.

The results will provide a clearer picture of Apple’s financial position and support better decision-making for management and investors.

## Company Background & Objectives

Apple Inc. operates in the global technology industry, producing consumer electronics, software, and services. This analysis will focus on the company’s most recent fiscal year.

The objective is to evaluate Apple’s financial health and identify key strengths and risks. Ratio analysis helps compare performance over time and against industry benchmarks, making it easier to understand how effectively the company is operating.

## Approach

Financial data will be collected from reliable sources, including the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (Form 10-K filings), Yahoo Finance, and Apple’s investor relations reports.

The analysis will include six ratio categories:

Performance: Overall return generated by the company
Profitability: Ability to generate earnings
Efficiency: How well assets are used to produce revenue
Leverage: Use of debt and financial risk
Liquidity: Ability to meet short-term obligations
Du Pont: Breakdown of return on equity to understand performance drivers
